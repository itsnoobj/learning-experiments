humandynamics.guide

## One-Liner

> Learn to see the patterns that drive people — through stories.

---

## The Problem

Every day you face situations no one taught you to handle:

- A coworker takes credit for your work
- A manager says one thing but means another
- A team resists a change that clearly benefits them
- A promotion goes to someone less qualified
- A relationship breaks down over something small

You're not bad at these situations. You just never learned to see what's actually driving them.

---

## The Insight

Behind every difficult situation are a handful of recurring forces:

**Incentives. Ego. Fear. Trust. Status. Identity. Scarcity. Power. Uncertainty. Reciprocity.**

These 10 forces explain almost everything — why people lie, resist, hoard, compete, deflect, avoid, and protect. Once you learn to see them, you can navigate anything.

---

## What We Are

A field guide to being human.

Not a course you finish. Not a book you read once. A reference you return to when you face something hard — at work, in relationships, in life.

Each chapter starts with a real situation, reveals the hidden force driving it, and teaches the pattern through stories — from the Mahabharata to Lincoln, from Stoic philosophy to modern psychology.

Then you test yourself. Apply it. Reflect. Move on sharper than before.

---

## How We Teach

Through stories, not lectures.

Every pattern is anchored in narrative — one from an ancient epic, one from history, one from everyday life. Because stories stick. Principles fade. But a story about Arjuna's hesitation, or Lincoln's patience, or a manager's blind spot — that stays with you.

Then: quizzes, reflection prompts, and multi-lens analysis (how would Krishna approach this? Munger? Marcus Aurelius?) to deepen understanding.

## The Logo

A 3×3 grid. Three gold squares on the diagonal. Six faint squares around them.

**The gold diagonal = the pattern you learn to see.**
**The faint squares = the forces that were always there but invisible.**

Before reading Human Dynamics, you see noise — confusing people, frustrating situations, unfair outcomes. After, you see the diagonal — the pattern was there all along. You just didn't know how to look.

## Brand Principles

1. **Situation-first.** Always start from "I'm facing X" — not "let me teach you Y."
2. **Stories over lectures.** Every principle anchored in narrative.
3. **Tension over answers.** Especially in ethics. Judgment grows from sitting with dilemmas.
4. **Open by default.** Content stays accessible. Premium is for experience, not knowledge.
5. **Honest, not nice.** We'd rather be useful than comfortable.
